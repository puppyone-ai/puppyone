Interview Notes — Customer Discovery
=====================================

Interviewee: Sarah Chen, Head of AI Platform @ FinTech Corp
Date: 2025-03-25
Duration: 35 min

Q: How many AI agents do you run in production?
A: "We have 12 agents across three teams. The compliance team alone has 4 —
   one for transaction monitoring, one for report generation, one for
   regulatory updates, and one for audit logging."

Q: What's your biggest pain point with agent data management?
A: "Honestly? It's when agents step on each other. Last month our report
   generator overwrote the audit agent's output because they shared a
   directory. Took us 3 hours to figure out what happened and recover."

Q: How do you currently handle agent permissions?
A: "We don't, really. Each agent has a service account with full S3 access.
   We know it's wrong but the alternative is writing custom IAM policies
   for every agent, which nobody has time for."

Q: If you could wave a magic wand, what would you want?
A: "Give each agent its own sandbox with clear boundaries. Like, this agent
   can only touch /compliance/reports/, period. And if something goes wrong,
   let me undo it in one click. Also, I want to know exactly what each agent
   did — for SOC 2 compliance we need full audit trails."

Q: Would you pay for that?
A: "Yesterday. We're spending $15k/month on a custom solution that does
   maybe 30% of what you described. And it breaks every other week."

---

Key Takeaways:
- Enterprise has 10+ agents, shared data is the #1 problem
- Compliance/audit is a buying trigger (not just nice-to-have)
- Current workaround: custom S3 + IAM = expensive and fragile
- Price sensitivity: low if it solves the problem reliably
- Best quote for marketing: "Give each agent its own sandbox with clear boundaries"
